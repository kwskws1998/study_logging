A paper reading note format

2026.06.29 5 min

A paper note that keeps only a summary can lose the source evidence later. I separate each claim, direct quotation, interpretation, and connection to my own work.

Basic unit

  • Claim: what the authors argue.
  • Evidence: a sentence copied directly from the paper.
  • Interpretation: what I think it means and where its limits are.
  • Action: what should change in my experiment or writing.

Put the excerpt first

For important points, paste the source sentence first and write the interpretation underneath it. This keeps evidence and inference separate when the paper is discussed later.

Metadata to keep

Keep the paper title, authors, publication year, venue, URL, and reading date together. When citing experimental results, also record the table or section number.
